Consolidation at constant strain rate

A simple manipulation of 1D TCT leads to the following governing equation in terms of axial strain \(\varepsilon_a\).
\( \cfrac{\partial \varepsilon_a}{\partial t}= \dot{\varepsilon_a}= C_v \cfrac{\partial^2 \varepsilon_a }{\partial z^2} \)
If \(\dot{\varepsilon}_a=r \equiv\) constant, then the solution of the equation simplifies and the test can be conducted in a short time.
Note: The RHS is still function of time because axial strains will be function of effective stresses \(\implies\) excess pore pressure.
The previous equation was solved by Wissa et al. (1971) assuming the boundary conditions on the right and:
  • Small strain theory
  • \(C_v\) constant with depth.
\( \varepsilon_a= \underbrace{ rt[1}_{\text{Av. strain}}+F(Z, T_v)]\)

With:
\( F= \underbrace{\frac{1}{6 T_v} (2 - 6Z +3 Z^2)}_{\text{Steady state solution}} -\underbrace{\frac{2}{\pi^2 T_v} \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{\cos (n \pi Z)}{n^2} \exp (-n^2 \pi^2 T_v )}_{\text{Transient state solution}} \)
  • The plot shows normalized axial strain with depth.
  • As soon as the loading starts a transient process occurs.
  • In order to interpret data from a CSR consolidation test, a stress-strain relationship is needed.

Solution at steady state

Steady state occurs for \(T_v \approx 0.4\). In this case, the last term of the solution vanishes.
\(F\): Analogous to the degree of consolidation.
\( F^k= 1 - \cfrac{\Delta u^k_{b} - \Delta u^0_k}{\sigma_v^k - \sigma_v^0 } \)

Upperscript \(k\) denotes the time step.

  • \(F^k < 0.4 \implies\) transient conditions.
  • \(F^k \geq 0.4 \implies\) steady state conditions.

Linear solutions

For steady state Wisa et al. (1971) proposed linear \(C_v=\) cte and non-linear solutions.

Effective stress

  1. At top \(\Delta u=0\) \(\implies \sigma'_{vt}= \sigma_v\)
  2. At bottom \(\Delta u_b \neq 0\) \(\implies\) \(\sigma'_{vb}=\sigma_v-\Delta u_b\)
  3. \(\Delta \sigma'= \Delta u_b\)
\(\sigma'_{av} \equiv\) Depth averaged effective stress
\( \sigma'_{av}= \sigma_v -\frac{2}{3} \Delta u_b \)

Other parameters

\( k= \frac{1}{2} \cfrac{rH^2 \gamma_w}{\Delta u_b} \)
\( m_v= r \cfrac{\Delta t}{\Delta \sigma_v} \)
\( C_v= \cfrac{H^2}{2 \Delta u_b} \left(\cfrac{\Delta \sigma_v}{\Delta t} \right) \)

Non-linear solutions

The depth-averaged effective stress for non-linear conditions is:
\( \sigma'_{av}= (\sigma_v^3 - 2 \sigma_v^2 \Delta u_b + \sigma_v \Delta u_b^2)^{1/3} \)
For the remainder parameters:
  1. Calculate \(C_v\):
    \( C_v = - \cfrac{H^2 \Delta \log \sigma_v}{ 2 \Delta t \log \left(1- \cfrac{\Delta u_b}{\sigma_v}\right)} \)
  2. Calculate \(m_v\):
    \( m_v= \cfrac{0.434 C_{\varepsilon}}{\sigma'_{av}} \)
  1. Calculate \(k\):
    \(k= C_v \gamma_w m_v \)
Note: These equations need to be discretized with your data.

Transient solutions

  • Wisa et al. (1971) contains solutions for transient conditions (\(F^k < 0.4 \)).
  • Transient conditions will develop during the initial times of loading or unloading stages.
  • Transient conditions are not included in the ASTM D4186.
  • You will not have to perform transient corrections for your laboratory report.
