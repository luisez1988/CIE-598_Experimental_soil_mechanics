Effective stress principle

The effective stress principle measures the stress arising from particle contacts. Soil behavior is controlled by effective stresses only.
:
\( \sigma'= \sigma - u \)

Where:
\( \sigma' \) = effective stress
\( \sigma \) = total stress
\( u \) = pore water pressure
How does pore pressure affect the state of stress in soils?

The principal stresses are:
\( \begin{align} \sigma'_1 &= \sigma_1 - u \\ \sigma'_3 &= \sigma_3 - u \\ \end{align} \)

The deviator stress:
\( \begin{align} \sigma_d&= \sigma'_1 - \sigma'_3 \\ &= (\sigma_1 - u) - (\sigma_3 - u) \\ &= \sigma_1 - \sigma_3 \end{align} \)

The deviator stress, and therefore, the shear stresses are independent of pore pressure.

Excess pore pressure

\(\Delta u\): Is the difference between the total pore pressure and the initial pore pressure.
\( \Delta u= u-u_0 \)
When soils are loaded excess pore pressure is generated. Thus, changes in total stresses produce \(\Delta u\).

Skempton's pore pressure parameters

In 1954, Skempton proposed two parameters to quantify \(\Delta u\). The so-called pore pressure parameters. Only valid for triaxial condition.
\( \Delta u= B \Delta \sigma_3 + \bar{A} (\Delta \sigma_1 - \Delta \sigma_3) \)

\( \begin{align} B= \frac{\Delta u_a}{\Delta \sigma_3} = \cfrac{1}{1+\frac{n C_v}{C_e}} \\ \end{align} \)
\(n=\) Porosity
\(C_v=\) compressibility of water
\(C_e=\) compressibility of soil

\( \begin{align} \bar{A}= \frac{\Delta u_d}{(\Delta \sigma_1 - \Delta \sigma_3)} \\ \end{align} \)

Since the compressibility of water is much larger than the compressibility of soil, \(B=1\) for saturated soils .

Example 1.1

A triaxial test was consolidated under a confining pressure of 100 kPa on a perfectly saturated sample. Subsequently, the cell pressure was increased by 20 kPa and a deviator stress of 50 kPa was applied. The pore pressure measured at the end of this loading stage was 10 kPa. Calculate Skempton's pore pressure parameters. Plot the total and effective stress Mohr circles.

An interpretation of excess pore pressure in clay

When the NC clay is loaded it will compress \(\longrightarrow\) it will generate positive excess pore pressure
Short-term conditions are critical. Design with total stress analysis.
When the OC is unloaded, it dilates. It will generate negative excess pore pressure
Long-term conditions are critical. Design with effective stress analysis.
