Course description

This course is designed to provide students with the knowledge and skills to characterize the mechanical behavior of soils. The course will cover the most common laboratory tests to determine the shear strength and consolidation parameters of soils. The course will also cover the most common in-situ tests to characterize soil behavior.

This course is:

  • Practice oriented
  • Lecture and laboratory components

References

Most of the reference for the course comes from journal articles. However, the following contains excellent information about testing and shear strength:

  • Bardet, J.-P. (1997). Experimental soil mechanics. Upper Saddle River, N.J: Prentice Hall.
  • Holtz, R.D., Kovacs, W.D. and Sheahan, T.C., 1981. An introduction to geotechnical engineering. Englewood Cliffs: Prentice-Hall.
  • Duncan, J.M., Wright, S.G. and Brandon, T.L., 2014. Soil strength and slope stability. John Wiley & Sons.

Course objectives

  1. To learn about the different tests to obtain shear strength and consolidation parameters of soils.
  2. To understand the effect of water drainage in soils to select the appropriate laboratory test and analysis type.
  3. To comprehend and learn the limitations and assumptions in the design and analysis of soil laboratory results.
  4. To understand the advantages and limitations of different in-situ tests and their usefulness for soil characterization.

Learning outcomes

  1. Describe sources of sampling disturbance.
  2. Describe the best practices for sample storage and preparation.
  3. Describe the assumptions, boundary conditions, and stress and strain paths of laboratory tests.
  4. Distinguish between drained and undrained analysis and total and effective parameters.
  5. Produce technical specifications for a laboratory test.
  6. Perform advanced laboratory testing.
  7. Critically review laboratory reports.
  8. Analyze in-situ test results.
  9. Design laboratory testing programs.

Course assessment

The course is graded as follows:
  • Laboratory practice: 20%
  • Homework and reports: 50%
  • Project: 30%

Grading scheme

  • A: Above 90
  • B: 80-90
  • C: 70-80
  • D: 60-70
  • F: Below 60

Laboratory practice

  • Seven lab practices
  • Your team is responsible for finishing the tests
  • You must read manuals and standards before the lab practice
  • You must keep the lab clean and organized
  • Grade is awarded in accordance with performance in the lab

Homework and reports

All homework will be submitted in Brighspace. All reports must be submitted in memo format as if you were writing to a client. The reports must be high quality and concise.
  • Reports are individual
  • Late reports will have a late penalty of 2 points per day
  • Reports without memo will not be graded

Tips for a good report

  • Be concise
  • Use appropriate symbols for units and variables
  • Label graphics correctly

Tips for a good report

  • Use correct Greek symbols for soil properties
  • In stress vs stress plots use same scale for x and y
  • Use dots for data and continuos lines for analytical results

Final Project

The project consists in writing a laboratory manual for any of the tests we will perform in the lab.
  • The project is individual
  • The grade is highly related to the quality of the manual
  • You will have a unique laboratory test of your choice.
  • Fill the Google Sheet to choose your lab
Tips for a good project:
  • Be concise
  • Use appropriate symbols for units and variables
  • Label graphics correctly
  • Always take pictures in your lab of everything

Tips for a good project

  • Submit an editable file
  • Attach a lab sheet
  • You must explain use of equipment
  • Explain the test step by step
  • Must explain how to get the raw data
  • Include list equipment and tools needed

Dos and don'ts in the lab

  • Do follow instructions and be actively engaged with material.
  • Do ask questions about anything at any time.
  • Do collaborate and work in teams.
  • Do be organized and clean as much as you can.
  • Do wear closed shoes for laboratory practices.
  • Do have fun!
  • Don't play with glassware or with other sharp tools.
  • Don't be distracted in the practice.
  • Don't be unprepared for the practice.
  • Don't distract your classmates during the practice.
  • Don't leave your samples unsupervised.
