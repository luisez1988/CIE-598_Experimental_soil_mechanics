Cone penetration test

The CPT is a push-in test that measures the resistance of the soil to a conical tip penetrating it.
  • The cone is advanced at constant rate of 2 cm/s.
  • Tip \(q_c\) and sleeve friction \(f_s\) resistance are measured.
  • Pore water pressure \(u_2\) is measured.
  • Measures are near-continuous.
  • ASTM D5778 available.

A brief history of CPT

  • First developed in the 30's as mechanical cone.
  • Electric cones developed in the 60's.
  • Prevalent in-situ method for off-shore investigations since the 70's.
  • Since the 70's major advancements include:
    • Pore pressure measurement (CPTu).
    • Seismic shear wave velocity (SCPTu).

Standards

There are many standards available for the CPT and CPTu.
  • ISSMGE - (IRTP) 1999/2001
  • USA - ASTM D5778
  • European: CEN TC341 for CPT and CPTu.
  • Other national standards and codes.
  • There are additional guidelines for offshore investigations.
The abundance of guidelines and standards ensures quality and reliability of the test.

Cone dimensions and definitions

A variety of cone configurations are possible. Below is a list of most common dimensions for cone penetrometers.
  • Cone apex angle \(\alpha\) 60 degrees .
  • Cone diameter: 37.5 mm for \(A_c=10 \text{cm}^2\).
    \(D=43.7\) mm for \(A_c=15 \text{cm}^2\).
  • Friction sleeve area: \(A_s= 150 \text{cm}^2\) for \(A_c=10 \text{cm}^2\) and \(A_s= 225 \text{cm}^2\) for \(A_c=15 \text{cm}^2\).
  • Common location for pore filter in CPTu is \(u_2\).
Other sensors can be mounted in the cone to measure additional parameters.

CPT tip resistance correction

It is the only correction done to the CPT data and it is due to pore pressure in the shoulder of the cone tip.
\(q_t\):
\( q_t= q_c + u_2 (1-a)\)

Where:
\(a= A_n/A_c \)
\(A_n \equiv\) area of the neck of the cone
Cones should have \(a > 0.75\)
Using SPT for soil characterization is like a doctor using a machete for a surgery.

Data repeatability and reliability

CPT sensors have accuracy about 0.1% to 0.5% of full scale output (FSO). This makes the CPT a repeatable and reliable test.
  • In general, tip resistance \(q_t\) is more accurate and repeatable than sleeve \(f_s\).
  • The use of separate separate load cells for the tip and sleeve forces improves reliability.
  • Tip \(q_t\) is more accurate and repeatable than \(u_2\), except in very soft fine-grained soils.
What are the factors that affect the repeatability of \(u_2\) results?
  • Filter saturation is a major factor.
  • saturation might be lost in the vadose zone (i.e., partially saturated zone).
  • Desaturation will occur for highly dilative materials because of the generation of negative excess pore pressure leading to cavitation.

Effect of thin soil layers

The tip resistance of strong thin layers surrounded by weak thick layers is underestimated.

\(R_f:\)
\( R_f= \frac{f_s}{q_t} \times 100\% \)
Incongruities at large depths in the original SBT chart required normalizing \(q_t\) to account for depth effects.
\(Q_t\):
\( Q_t= \frac{q_t-\sigma_{vo}}{\sigma'_{vo}} \)

\(\sigma_{vo} \equiv\) Total vertical stress
\(\sigma'_{vo} \equiv\) Effective vertical stress

\(F\):
\( F= \frac{f_s}{q_t-\sigma_{v_o}} \times 100\% \)
\(B_q\):
\( B_q= \Delta u/ q_n \)

\(\Delta u= u_2 - u_0 \equiv\) Excess pore pressure.
\(q_n= q_t- \sigma_{vo} \equiv\) Net cone resistance.

\(I_c\):
\( I_c= \sqrt{(3.47- \log Q_t)^2 + (\log F_r + 1.22)^2} \)

Determining \(S_u\) from CPT

Equation based on bearing capacity theory.
\( S_u = \cfrac{q_t - \sigma_v}{N_{kt}} \)

\(N_{kt} \equiv \) Bearing capacity factor between 10-20.

  • Average \(N_{kt}=\)14
  • \(\uparrow IP \implies \uparrow N_{kt}\)
  • \(\uparrow S_t \implies \downarrow N_{kt}\)
  • \(\uparrow B_q \implies \downarrow N_{kt}\)
  • For \(B_q \approx 1\) \(N_{kt}\) can be as low as 6.

In very soft clays with some uncertainty on the accuracy in \(q_t\), \(S_u\) can be estimated from excess pore pressure \(\Delta u\).
\( S_u= \cfrac{\Delta u}{N_{\Delta u}} \)

\(N_{\Delta u}= B_q N_{kt} \)

  • \(N_{\Delta u}\) varies from 4 to 8.
  • For a conservative estimate, select a value close to the upper limit
  • If experience is available in the same deposit, you can fit \(N_{\Delta u}\)
