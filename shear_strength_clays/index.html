Drained shear strength of soils

Using a classic view of shear strength (\(\tau_f=S\)), we can calculate it using the Mohr-Coulomb model.
\( \tau_f = S= c' + \sigma'_n \tan \phi' \)

where: \(c' \equiv\) effective cohesion, \(\sigma'_n \equiv\) effective normal stress, and \(\phi' \equiv\) effective angle of internal friction.
Note: More sophisticated models exist but are covered in great detail in advanced courses.
However, the Mohr-Coulomb model is still widely used and facilitates understanding soil behavior.
How can we get drained behavior of clay❓
Recall drained means \(\Delta u= 0\) and clay has a very low permeability.
Drained behavior of clay is obtained by allowing the pore water to flow out of the soil during shearing. This is done using drained boundary conditions and very slow rates of loading.

The shear strength of clay is function of:
  1. The OCR. \(\uparrow OCR \implies \uparrow \tau_f\).
  2. The confinement stress. \(\uparrow \sigma'_3 \implies \uparrow \tau_f\)
  3. The density. \(\uparrow e_o \implies \downarrow \tau_f\)
To a lesser degree \(\tau_f\) is function of:
  • Fabric.
  • Mineralogy.
  • Rate of loading.

Strength of NC and OC clay

If peak strength is selected as criteria of failure the following occurs:
  1. \(\phi'_{NC}> \phi'_{OC}\)
  2. For NC clay: \(c'=0\)
  3. For OC clay: \(c'_{OC}>0\)
Is the strength equivalent to the peak strength of a normally consolidated remolded specimen.
How does the softening process occur❓
The process of softening occurs due to degradation and/or destructuration of the soil fabric. It can happen due to:
  • Repeated wetting and drying cycles in OC clays lead to cracks. The overall effect is a natural remolding process.
  • Creep mobilizes deformations that trigger softening by a gradual destructuration of the soil fabric.
  • Change of effective stress. This clays can rapidly transfer load producing retrogressive failure.
What is the practical use of FSSS❓
The FSSS is used in practice in limit equilibrium analyses. Eg. slope stability, bearing capacity, etc.

Use for:

  • Slope stability of cut slopes in heavily overconsolidated clay.
  • Used for long-term drained analysis.
  • Producing safer analysis than using peak conditions.

Note: The use of modern computational tools and advanced constitutive models made the use of FSSS unnecessary.
