critical state theory

Critical state theory is a useful framework to describe the behavior of soils. It is based on the concept that at large strains, soils tend to evolve towards a critical state that is independent of the initial conditions.
Idea #1: At equal mean stress, materials with different initial density have equal ultimate strength.
\( q_c= M_c p'_c \)

\(M_c=\) critical friction ratio
Idea #2: At equal mean stress, materials with different densities have equal ultimate void ratio also called critical void ratio.
\( e_{c}(p')= \Gamma - \lambda \ln (p') \)
The equations from ideas #1 and #2 are used to define the critical state line.

critical state axioms

Apart from establishing the critical state line, critical state theory also provides a set of axioms used to describe the behavior of soils.
At large deformation, the deviatoric stress is steady.
\(\lim_{\varepsilon_q \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\partial q}{\partial \varepsilon_q}=0 \)
At large deformation, the mean stress is steady.
\(\lim_{\varepsilon_q \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\partial p}{\partial \varepsilon_q}=0 \)
At large deformation, there is no volume change.
\(\lim_{\varepsilon_q \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\partial \varepsilon_v}{\partial \varepsilon_q}=D=0 \)

Stress-dilation

Now, we will use the energy density equation to derive a theoretical relationship between stress and dilation.

\( \begin{align} \dot{W}^p&= q \dot{\varepsilon}^p_q + p' \dot{\varepsilon}^p_v \\ \cfrac{\dot{W}^p}{p' \dot{\varepsilon}_q^p} &= \cfrac{q}{p'} + \cfrac{\dot{\varepsilon}_v^p}{\dot{\varepsilon}_q^p} \\ \cfrac{\dot{W}^p}{p' \dot{\varepsilon}_q^p} &= \eta + D^p\\ \end{align} \)

Where \( \eta \) is the stress ratio. Now, take the limit when \( \dot{\varepsilon}_q^p \rightarrow \infty \).

\( \begin{align} \lim_{\dot{\varepsilon}_q^p \rightarrow \infty} \cfrac{\dot{W}^p}{p' \dot{\varepsilon}_q^p} &= \lim_{\dot{\varepsilon}_q^p \rightarrow \infty} ( \eta + D^p ) \\ \lim_{\dot{\varepsilon}_q^p \rightarrow \infty} \cfrac{\dot{W}^p}{p' \dot{\varepsilon}_q^p} &= M_c \end{align} \)

At first Roscoe and Schofield proposed that \( M_c \) was a constant. This lead to the development of the Camclay model. Using this proposition we obtain:
\( M_c= \eta + D^p \)

State parameter

\( \psi:\) Is the vertical distance between the current void ratio and the critical void ratio.
\( \psi= e- e_c \)
