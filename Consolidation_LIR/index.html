Consolidation

: Is the process of densification as water flows out of the soil mass as a response to an increase in total stresses. It is a time dependent process.

Terzaghi's 1D consolidation theory

Therzaghi's 1D consolidation theory (TCT) is based on mass balance concepts.

The following assumptions are made:

  1. The soil is isotropic and homogeneous
  2. The soil compressibility is linear
  3. The grains and the water are incompressible
  4. Darcy's law is valid

The derivation leads to:
\(\frac{\partial u}{\partial t}= C_v \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial z^2} \)
\( \begin{align} C_v=k_z / m_v \gamma_w \end{align} \)
\(C_v=\) Coefficient of consolidation
\(k_z=\) Permeability in the vertical direction
\(m_v=\) Modulus of volume compressibility
\(\gamma_w=\) Unit weight of water
\(u=\) Excess pore water pressure
TCT theory has analytical solution for the following boundary conditions:
  1. Initial condition: \(u(z, 0)=q\)
  2. Boundary condition: \(u(0, t)=0\)
  3. Boundary condition: \(u(H, t)=0\)
\( \begin{align} u(z,t) = q \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{2}{M} \sin\left(M Z\right) \exp\left(-M^2 T_v\right) \end{align} \)

\( \begin{align} T_v=\frac{t}{C_v H_{dr}^2} \end{align} \)
\(q=\) Surcharge load
\(M= (\pi/2)(2n+1)\)
\(Z= z / H_{dr}\)
\(H_{dr}=\) drainage path length
Representation of the consolidation process in a single element of soil.
\(U\): It helps understand the progress of the consolidation process in the whole system.
\( \begin{align} U(t) = 1 - \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{2}{M^2} \exp\left(-M^2 T_v\right) \end{align} \)

Secondary consolidation

Also known as creep, is a time-dependent process of deformation that occurs without change of effective stress. Do not confuse with the primary consolidation described by the TCT.
Note: Creep could be ongoing before EOP.
