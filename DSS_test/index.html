Direct simple shear test

Involves uniform shear of material fibers in one direction parallel to a horizontal plane of the specimen. Simple shear occurs at constant volume and the shear plane is not predefined.

Disturbance effects

Recall the Ladd and DeGroot (2004) paper. The sampling effective stress is influenced by the disturbance of the sample.
The sampling stress is function of:
  • Disturbance
  • Sample depth
  • Soil type

The \(S_{u,UU}\) is always smaller than \(S_{u,field}\).

Saturation effects

Recall that undrained implies no volume change. A partially saturated sample will change volume and behave partially drained.
How does an unconfined compressive test differs from a UUTx‚ùì
  • Water can drain out of sample. Implies change of volume is allowed.
  • Saturation is not controlled.
The \(S_{u,UU}\) is always greater than \(S_{u,UCT}\). Do not use UCT results for design.

Steps required for a UUTx test

The following is a brief list of the steps required for a UUTx test.
  1. Check apparatus state and calibrations.
  2. Extract and prepare sample.
  3. Weight and measure specimen.
  4. Mount the sample on the pedestal.
  1. Close pore pressure valve and increase \(\sigma_3\) to desired level.
  2. With pore pressure valve closed, shear the sample.
  3. Unmount and measure final state.
  4. Repeat at least three times.
  5. Post-process the data.

Strain rate selection

  • The strain rate shall be chosen so that the time to "failure" does not exceed 15 minutes.
  • Select a strain rate between 0.3 to 1%/min.
  • Softer or plastic materials will exhibit larger deformation at failure. As a result should be tested in the higher range of strain rates.
  • Stiff or brittle materials will exhibit small deformations at failure and should be tested at the lower range of strain rates.

Determining the cell pressures

The cell pressure should be similar to in-situ conditions total stresses prior to the application of any external load.

Post processing

The post processing calculations are identical to CUTx calculations. If \(S \approx 100\%\), use the average \(S_u\) from the three tests.

Pros and cons

PROS

  • It is a fast test to perform.
  • Simple mounting and interpretation.

CONS

  • Results are highly depending on saturation and disturbance
  • Sample is not back pressured.
  • Pore pressure is not monitored.
  • Can't get effective stress parameters.
