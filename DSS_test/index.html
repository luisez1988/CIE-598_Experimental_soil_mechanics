Steps required for a DSS test

The following is a brief list of the steps required for a DSS test.
  1. Check apparatus state and calibrations.
  2. Extract and prepare sample.
  3. Weight and measure specimen.
  4. Mount the sample on the pedestal.
  5. Consolidate sample to desired stress level.
  1. Calculate rate of shear.
  2. shear the sample. Modify rate to ensure \(\Delta u \approx 0\).
  3. Unmount and measure final state.
  4. Repeat at least three times.
  5. Post-process the data.

Rate of shear selection

  • The maximum strain rate should result in specimen failure in around twice the time for 90% of consolidation.
    \( \dot{\gamma} \leq \cfrac{2 \gamma_f}{t_{90}} \)
  • Use \(\gamma_f \approx\) 4 to 10 %
  • Much of the existing data and practical experience have been developed using a shear strain rate of 5% per hour (ASTM D6528).
  • During shearing, the strain rate can be adjusted to reduce excess pore pressure generation.

Determining the vertical stresses

The vertical stresses should be similar to in-situ effective stresses prior to the application of any external load.

The post processing calculations are identical to CUTx calculations. If \(S \approx 100\%\), use the average \(S_u\) from the three tests.

Pros and cons

PROS

  • It is a fast test to perform.
  • Simple mounting and interpretation.

CONS

  • Results are highly depending on saturation and disturbance
  • Sample is not back pressured.
  • Pore pressure is not monitored.
  • Can't get effective stress parameters.
