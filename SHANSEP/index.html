What is SHANSEP?

SHANSEP stands for Stress History and Normalized Soil Engineering Properties. It is a laboratory protocol to reduce/remove the disturbance effects in fine-grained samples.

  1. Allows to characterize the undrained shear strength ratio and over consolidation history.
  2. SHANSEP is not an equation.
  3. SHANSEP is not a constitutive equation.

  • Originally proposed by Ladd and Foott in 1974.
  • Two key aspects behind theory:
    1. Stress-strain normalization.
    2. re-setting/erasing sample disturbance by loading to virgin curve.
  • Required tests to be performed:
    1. Consolidation tests to asses \(OCR\) profile.
    2. Minimum 6 \(CK_oDSS\) or \(CK_oUTC\) tests.
    3. Can be done with DST as well.

Theoretical Considerations

Let's start defining the \(USR\) using critical state.
This results into a familiar expression for the \(USR\):
\( \left(\cfrac{S_u}{p'_0}\right)= \cfrac{M_c}{2} \left(\cfrac{R}{2} \right)^{\Lambda} \)

Similar to:
\( \left(\cfrac{S_u}{\sigma'_{v0}}\right)= k \ OCR^b\)

Other versions of the USR equation

Soil Type Equation Reference
NC clay \[ \frac{s_u}{\sigma'_{rz}} = 0.11 + 0.0037 \text{PI} \] Skempton (1957)
NC clay \[ \frac{s_u}{\sigma'_{rzo}} = 0.22 \] Mesri (1975)
OC clay \[ \frac{s_u}{\sigma'_{rz}}_{oc} = \frac{s_u}{\sigma'_{rz}}_{nc} \cdot \text{OCR}^{0.8} \] Ladd et al. (1977)
OC clay \[ \frac{s_u}{\sigma'_{rz}} = \left(0.23 \pm 0.04\right) \cdot \text{OCR}^{0.8} \] Jamiolkowski et al. (1985)

Normalized behavior

Assumption that at equal OCR, the normalized stress-strain curves are the same for a given soil.

Stress history

Specimens are loaded to the virgin curve to remove disturbance effects.
