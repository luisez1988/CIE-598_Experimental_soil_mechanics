Triaxial stress-state

Triaxial stress-states exist when a material is subjected to three principal stresses. In a true triaxial, the three principal stresses are normal stresses acting on three mutually perpendicular planes.

The triaxial cell

  • It has two hydraulic systems. One for controlling the cell pressure, and the other for the specimen drainage.
  • Measures axial load and deformation, and volumetric change or pore pressure.
  • Can obtain more information (e.g., radial strain, wave propagation).

Consolidated drained triaxial test

  • Can be saturated with back pressure.
  • First stage is consolidation to desired stress level.
  • Second stage is shearing at sufficiently slow rate to ensure \(\Delta u \approx 0\).
  • Drainage valves are open to allow volume change.

A ICDTx test does not rotate principal stresses

\(K_f\) line

Is the Mohr-Coulomb line in \(\sigma_d - \sigma'_m\) space.
\( \sin \phi'= \tan \alpha \)
\( d'= c' \cos \phi'\)

Other variables of interest

\(D\): Is the ratio of change of volumetric strain to change of deviatoric strain.
\( D= \cfrac{d \varepsilon_v}{d \varepsilon_q}= \cfrac{\dot{\varepsilon}_v}{\dot{\varepsilon}_q}\)
For a triaxial state:
\( \varepsilon_q= \varepsilon_a- \frac{\varepsilon_v}{3} \)
Can be calculated from a \(\varepsilon_v\) vs. \(\varepsilon_q\) dataset. Use centered approximation with a span of 2 or 4.
\(PSR\): Is the ratio of the maximum to minium principal stresses.
\( PSR= \cfrac{\sigma'_1}{\sigma'_3} \)
At failure: \(PSR_f= \tan^2 \left(45+ \cfrac{\phi'}{2}\right)\)
\(\eta\): Is the ratio of the mean to deviatoric stress.
\( \eta= q/p' \)

At failure: \(\eta_f= \cfrac{6 \sin \phi'}{3- \sin \phi'}\)

Steps required for a ICDTx test

The following is a brief list of the steps required for a ICDTx test. You will gain detailed knowledge in your laboratory practice.
  1. Check apparatus state and calibrations.
  2. Extract and prepare sample.
  3. Weight and measure specimen.
  4. Mount the sample on the pedestal.
  5. Saturate using back pressure.
  1. Consolidate sample to desired \(\sigma'_{3CON}\).
  2. shear the sample.
  3. Unmount and measure final state.
  4. Repeat at least three times.
  5. Post-process the data.

Back pressure saturation

Close the pore pressure valve. Then increase the cell pressure and calculate:
\( B= \cfrac{\Delta u}{\Delta \sigma_{cell}} \)
  • Simultaneously increase the cell and pore pressure in steps with valves open.
  • Avoid prestressing the sample by allowing enough time for pressure equalization.
  • Use pressure increments from 35 kPa to 140 kPa (5 psi to 20 psi)
  • The difference between cell and pore pressure should not exceed 35 kPa (5 psi)
  • \(B \geq 0.95\) to ensure saturation.

Consolidation stage

Allows the specimen to reach equilibrium in a drained state at the effective consolidation stress \(\sigma'_{3CON}\) for which a strength determination is required
  • Data is used to compute the strain rate for the shear stage.
  • With valve closed, hold back pressure, and increase cell pressure until difference is equal to desired consolidation pressure.
  • Open valve and allow consolidation.
  • Ensure EOP is achieved.
  • Record axial deformations, height, and area before shearing.

Calculating the strain rate

The strain rate must be calculated to ensure \(\Delta u \approx 0\) during the shear stage.
  • If specimen has side drain:
    \( \dot{\varepsilon}_a= \cfrac{4 \%}{16 t_{90}} \)
  • Otherwise:
    \( \dot{\varepsilon}_a= \cfrac{4 \%}{10 t_{90}} \)

Determining the consolidation stresses

Conduct the tests at stress levels similar to those expected at long-term conditions.

Post processing

The post processing calculations are well detailed in the ASTM D7181. Here we will revise some of the criteria to correct the data.

To calculate stresses, you need to account for change of cross sectional area.

\( A= \cfrac{V_c - \Delta V_{\varepsilon}}{H_c - \Delta H_{\varepsilon}} \)
\(V_c \equiv\) Sample's volume after consolidation
\(\Delta V_{\varepsilon} \equiv\) change of volume at current strain
\(H_c \equiv\) Sample's height after consolidation
\(\Delta H_{\varepsilon} \equiv\) change of height at current strain
For calculating the acting deviator stress the ASTM D7181 add the piston, cap, and half sample weight. Besides it adds the load acting on the difference of area between the piston and sample.
\( \sigma_d= \cfrac{P + K + (A-a) \sigma_3}{A} - \sigma_3 \)
\(P \equiv\) Load on the piston
\(K \equiv\) Additional weight
\(A \equiv\) Area of the sample
\(a \equiv\) Area of the piston
Check the ASTM D7181 for detailed definition of \(K\).
