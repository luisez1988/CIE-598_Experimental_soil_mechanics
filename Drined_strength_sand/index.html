Soil Dilatancy

Soil dilatancy is the increase in void volume of a soil mass when it is sheared. It has a significant effect on the shear strength of soils.

Observed patterns in the drained behavior of sand at equal \(\sigma'_3\):
  • The peak deviator stress of dense sands is larger than for loose sand.
  • The ultimate deviator stress of all tests converges to a critical value.
  • The ultimate void ratio of all tests converges to a critical value.
At large strains, shear occurs atconstant volume.
\(\psi_0\): Vertical distance from the initial to the critical void ratio.
\( \psi_0= e_0 - e_c \)
  • If \(\psi_0>0 \implies\) compression.
  • If \(\psi_0<0 \implies\) dilation.
The critical state line provides a useful framework for the interpretation of soil behavior.
This knowledge is important to validate test results.

Interpretation using Mohr-Coulomb

  • At peak conditions \(\phi'_p \equiv\) peak friction angle.
  • Can calculate secant friction angles \(\phi'_s\). Eg. using 10% of strain as reference.
  • The critical friction angle \(\phi'_c\) is reflective of pure inter-particle friction.
  • Cohesionless. Cohesion arises from cementation only.
What factors affect the friction angle of sands‚ùì
  • \(\uparrow\) Density \(\implies \uparrow \phi'\)
  • \(\uparrow\) confining pressure \(\implies \downarrow \phi'\)
  • Particle shape: \(\uparrow\) angularity \(\implies \uparrow \phi'\)
  • \(\uparrow\) particle size \(\implies \downarrow \phi'\)
  • \(\uparrow\) gradation \(\implies \uparrow \phi'\)
  • Mineralogy controls base coefficient of friction.

Correlations for friction angle

In practice, estimating the friction angle is essential and often done through correlations with more accessible properties like relative density, which also help validate lab results.

The following expression was proposed by Duncan et al. (2014):

\( \phi'= a+bD_r-[c+d D_r] \log_{10} \left( \cfrac{\sigma'_n}{P_a}\right) \)

\(a\), \(b\), \(c\), and \(d\) are regression coefficients. \(D_r\) is the relative density, \(\sigma'_n\) is the normal stress, and \(P_a\) is the atmospheric pressure.

The coefficients for Duncan's equation can be found below:
Material \(a\) (deg) \(b\) (deg) \(c\) (deg) \(d\) (deg) Standard Deviation (deg)
Gravel and cobbles with \(C_u>4\) 44 10 7 2 3.1
Sand with \(C_u>6\) 39 10 3 2 3.2
Sand with \(C_u \leq 6\) 34 10 3 2 3.2

Take away...

  • The drained behavior of sand is affected by the initial void ratio and confining pressure.
  • At equal confinement pressure a critical state is attained at large axial strains.
  • Soils with initial state parameter \(\psi_0>0\) will compress.
  • Soils with initial state parameter \(\psi_0<0\) will dilate.
  • Peak, secant, and critical friction angles can be interpreted from tests.
