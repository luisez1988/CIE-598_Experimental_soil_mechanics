Objective of the direct shear test

  • Measure effective parameters such as \(c'\), \(\phi'_p\), \(\phi'_r\).
  • Consolidation parameters can also be obtained.
  • \(C_v\) must be measured to determine shear rate.

Pros and cons of the DS test

Pros
  • Is a \(K_0\) consolidated test
  • Plane-strain test
  • Conservative if sheared parallel to sedimentation plane
  • Simple to mount and perform
  • Provides consolidation results
  • ASTM provided
Cons
  • Does not provide undrained shear strengths
  • Does not provide strains
  • Modulus and stress-strain relationships cannot be obtained
  • Pore pressures are not measured
  • Stress paths are unknown
  • Progressive failure

Sample preparation-Sand

  • Sand must be deposited with care using a consistent procedure.
  • Ensure that the test can be replicated. Typically conduct tests under identical settings at least twice.
  • Should try to have uniform void ratio trough the sample
  • Various methos available:
    1. Pluviation
    2. Sedimentation
    3. Funnel deposition
    4. Controlled compaction or vibration
  • Need max and min void ratio to determine relative density
  • All samples should have the same initial void ratio

Sample preparation- Clay

  • Intact samples are trimmed first following bets practices.
  • For fully softened shear strength follow the following procedure:
    1. Soak soil for two days
    2. Sieve through sive No. 40
    3. Air-dry the sample
    4. Sample should be near the liquid limit for testing. 24-26 blows in a Casagrande cup.
  • Place the bottom place with saturated porous stone and filter paper.
  • For FSSS fill using a spatula from outside to inside. Press to remove air.

Sample preparation- Clay

  • Clean and put top box and black screws. Repeat filling procedure.
  • Clean excess soil and place the gap screws (yellow screws).

ASTM D3080 details

  • Withdrawn in April 2020.
  • 2:1 minimum test specimen diameter or width to thickness ratio required.
  • During shear, readings should be taken at displacement intervals of 2% of specimen diameter or width.
  • Follow recomendations presented in ASTM D2435 for the consolidation stage.
  • Weight of top half of the DS box should be less than 1% of the applied normal load. Otherwise it should be supported by a counter force.

Problems in a DS test

  • End of primary consolidation not reached. It is very important to ensure EOP for last consolidation load.
  • Sample sheared too fast. Test will not be a drained test.
  • Shearing stage stop early. The test specimen should past the peak shear stress to get a well defined curve.

Specifying a DS test

  • Normal stress is selected based on problem being analyse. Could perform a sacrifitail analysis to find a range of normal stress for testing.
  • Shear rate is based on the \(C_v\). Use Taylor's or Casagrande's method and calculate the displacement rate using: $$d_r=\cfrac{d_f}{50 t_{50}}=\cfrac{d_f}{11.6 t_{90}}$$
  • \(d_r=\) displacement rate, \(d_f=\) final displacement (Use 0.1 in for NC clays and 0.2 in otherwise).
