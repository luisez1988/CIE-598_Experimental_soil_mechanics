Perfect sampling

An intact sample undergoes several processes that can disturb it. Therefore, the concept of perfect sampling is an idealization.
We can use Skempton's pore pressure parameters to approximate the perfect sampling effective stress.
\(\sigma'_{ps}\):
\( \sigma'_{ps}= \sigma'_{h,0} \)

Disturbance in the lab

The sample effective stress is much lower than \(\sigma'_{ps}\).
As a result, the undrained shear strength that would be obtained if the sample had the in-situ initial conditions is larger than the laboratory value.

Read: "Recommended practice for soft ground site characterization": Arthur Casagrande Lecture

Drilling

Depending on exploration depth and location there is a diverse set of equipment available. During drilling, vertical stress is reduced compared to horizontal stress causing triaxial extension (TE).
Drilling mud prevents failure under TE providing additional vertical stress.

Tube sampling

Sample disturbance is reduced with:
  • Increasing sampler diameter
  • Decreasing sampler thickness

Use outer diameter 76 mm if tube sample is 75 mm (\(D_0/t>45\)) with sharp cutting edge with inner clearance ratio \(ICR\approx 0\%\).
\(ICR=(D_i-D_c)/D_c\)

Tube extraction

  • Intact soil below the sampler will resist extraction.
  • As the sample is moved to the surface, pore pressure is reduced \(\longrightarrow\) gas exsolution may occur.
  • Seal the tube as soon as possible.
  • Hydraulic piston sampler produces the lowest amount of disturbance. ASTM available (ASTM D6519).

Transportation and storage

Sample transportation must be done carefully to avoid further disturbance. You want to preserve the direction of the gravity loading.
  • Store tubes in vertical position.
  • Tubes can be stored horizontal only temporally.
The ASTM standard D4220 provides guidelines for transportation and storage of soil samples.

Sample extrusion

  • Don't use metallic tubes for long-term storage. Soil will be chemically improved.
  • Significant disturbance can be produced during extrusion. Cracks could develop.
  • If possible, follow procedure suggested in Ladd and DeGrout (2003)

Long-term storage

It is good practice to extract samples from Shelby tubes as soon as possible. This is important for long-term storage (e.g. > 1 month).
Follow the following procedures:
  1. Take samples for moisture content and shear strength indices
  2. Usually separated in 12" chunks. Log depths carefully
  3. Wrap in saran wrap followed by aluminum paper
  4. Place in plastic container and wax

Handling/trimming

Don't break the sample

Don't let sample bend and and break

Don't let the sample bend or squish the sample

Use a wire saw and slide the sample

Use a wire saw and catcher

Use a solid base and keep the sample vertical

Sampling coarse materials

In practice, obtaining intact samples from coarse materials is challenging. We often rely on empirical correlations to estimate strength and permeability properties.
In some cases we are interested in testing coarse materials (e.g., for cyclic loading and liquefaction). In these cases, samples must be reconstituted at different densities to characterize behavior under a wide range of conditions.

Sample reconstitution techniques

About ASTM

FACTS about ASTM

  • Over 12,500 standards
  • Over 140 participating countries
  • 80+ volumes of ASTM book of standards
  • Manages the Geotechnical Testing Journal

  • Hazards

  • Preparation of apparatus
  • Calibration and standardization
  • Conditioning
  • Procedure
  • Calculation and results interpretation
  • Precision and Bias

  • Keywords
  • Annexes and Appendixes
  • References
  • Summary of changes

Take away

  • Cohesive soil sampling inevitably disturbs samples. Refer to well treated samples as "intact" instead of "undisturbed."
  • Sample disturbance is minimized following quality protocols and standards from the drilling process to sample preparation and testing.
  • Samples of coarse materials must be reconstituted.
  • ASTM standards are essential for geotechnical testing. They provide guidelines for sampling, testing, and reporting.
  • Good geotechnical experimental testing is 10% solid knowledge and 90% skills (T. Brandon VT).
