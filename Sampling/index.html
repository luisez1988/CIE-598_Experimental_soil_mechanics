Perfect sampling

Disturbance in the lab

Soil testing involves:

  • Intrusion of sampler in the soil
  • Extraction of sampler
  • Handling of sample in-situ
  • Transportation of sample
  • Handling sample in the lab
  • Preparing the sample for testing

Read: "Recommended practice for soft ground site characterization": Arthur Casagrande Lecture

Drilling

  • Depending on exploration depth and location there is a diverse set of equipment available.
  • During drilling, vertical stress is reduced compared to horizontal stress causing triaxial extension (TE).
  • Drilling mud prevents failure under TE providing additional vertical stress.

Tube sampling

Sample disturbance is reduced with:
  • Increasing sampler diameter
  • Decreasing sampler thickness

Use outer diameter 76 mm if tube sample is 75 mm \(D_0/t>45\) with sharp cutting edge with inner clearance ratio \(ICR\approx 0\%\). \(ICR=(D_i-D_c)/D_c\)

Tube extraction

  • Intact soil below the sampler will resist extraction.
  • As the sample is moved to the surface, pore pressure is reduced \(\longrightarrow\) gas exsolution may occur.
  • Seal the tube as soon as possible.
  • Hydraulic piston sampler produces the lowest amount of disturbance. ASTM available (ASTM D6519).

Transportation and storage

  • Tubes are stored in vertical possition.
  • Tubes can be stored horizontal only temporally.
  • Follow ASTM D4220.

Sample extrussion

  • Don't use samples metalic tubes for long-term storage. Soil will be chemically improved.
  • Significant disturbance can be produced during extrussion. Cracks could develop.
  • Cut tubes with horizontal band saw. Follow procedure suggested in Ladd and DeGrout (2003)

Long-term storing

  • Take moisture content samples and shear strength indeces if possible
  • Usually separeted in 12" samples. Log depths carefully
  • Wrap in saran wrap
  • Wrap in aluminium wrap
  • Place in plastic container
  • Wax

Handling/triming

Don't breake the sample

Don't let sample bend and and breake

Don't let the sample bend. Don't squish the sample

Use a wire saw and slide the sample

Use a wire saw and catcher

Use a solid base and move the sample verticallyf

Take away

  • Cohesive soil sampling inhebitably disturbs samples. Refer to well treated samples as "intact" instead of "undisturbed"
  • Sample disturbance is minimized following quality protocols and standards from the drilling process to sample preparation and testing.
  • Good geotechnical experimental testing is 10% solid knowledge and 90% skills (T. Brandon VT).
